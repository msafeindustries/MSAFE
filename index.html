Core Systems

What's actually inside.

Five mechanisms, one board.

01

Dry-Run Protection

Magnetic sensors detect zero-flow and low-source conditions instantly, cutting power before the motor overheats or the windings burn out.

02

Voltage Management

Dynamic monitoring shields the motor from surges, drops and phase irregularities on the incoming supply.

03

Magnetic Float Sensing

Corrosion-resistant floats built for years of maintenance-free switching, even in hard water.

04

Overflow Prevention

A precise upper cutoff stops the tank from wasting water once it's full — no timers, no guesswork.

05

Anti-Cycling Lockout

A built-in restart delay stops rapid on/off switching during unstable supply, which is what actually kills most pump motors.

How It Works

Tank to switch, four steps.

Operation and installation.

01

Sensing

Magnetic floats track the water level continuously, without electronics touching the water.

02

Decision

The controller checks live level data against your set high and low points.

03

Switching

The relay engages or cuts power to the pump, with a delay built in against rapid cycling.

04

Protection

A dry-run or overflow condition overrides the cycle and shuts the pump down immediately.

01

Mount the Unit

Fix the controller near the pump switch or distribution board.

02

Place the Floats

Position the magnetic floats at your desired high and low marks.

03

Wire the Pump

Connect the pump supply through the relay per the wiring guide.

04

Power On

Switch on the mains — operation starts immediately, no setup required.
